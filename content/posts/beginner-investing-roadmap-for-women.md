Most women who are not investing yet are not avoiding it because they do not care about building wealth. They are avoiding it because nobody has ever explained it in plain language without making them feel behind or foolish.

Consider this that explanation.

## Step One: Get Your Financial Foundation in Place First

Before you invest a single dollar, you need two things. An emergency fund with at least one thousand dollars, ideally three to six months of expenses. And no high-interest debt, meaning credit card balances with interest rates above eight percent.

Investing while carrying twenty-four percent credit card interest is mathematically backwards. Pay the high-interest debt first. Then invest.

## Step Two: Understand What Investing Actually Is

Investing is buying something that you expect to grow in value over time.

When you buy a stock, you are buying a small piece of a company. When that company grows and becomes more valuable, your piece becomes more valuable too. When you buy an index fund, you are buying a tiny piece of hundreds of companies at once, which spreads your risk enormously.

You do not need to understand every company in an index fund. You just need to understand that you own a small slice of a lot of businesses, and historically, businesses grow over time.

## Step Three: Open a Roth IRA

A Roth IRA is the single best starting point for most women who are new to investing.

Here is why. You contribute money that has already been taxed. It grows tax-free inside the account. When you withdraw it in retirement, you pay no tax on any of the growth. That tax-free growth over decades is an enormous advantage.

You can open a Roth IRA at Fidelity, Vanguard, or Charles Schwab. All three are free to open and have no minimum balance requirement to start.

## Step Four: Choose Simple Index Funds

Once your account is open, you need to choose what to invest in. The answer for almost every beginner is index funds.

An index fund tracks a market index like the S&P 500, which is made up of the five hundred largest companies in the United States. When you buy an S&P 500 index fund, you automatically own a tiny piece of five hundred companies.

Look for funds with low expense ratios. Fidelity's FZROX and Vanguard's VTSAX are popular options. The expense ratio is the annual fee charged by the fund. Lower is always better. Aim for under 0.2 percent.

## Step Five: Automate Your Contributions

Set up automatic monthly contributions to your Roth IRA. Even fifty dollars a month to start. The 2024 contribution limit for a Roth IRA is seven thousand dollars per year, which works out to about five hundred and eighty dollars a month.

Contribute what you can now and increase it as your income grows. The most important thing is starting, not the amount.

## Step Six: Leave It Alone

This is the step most new investors fail.

The stock market goes up and down. Sometimes it goes down significantly. When that happens, every instinct you have will tell you to pull your money out before it drops further.

Do not.

Long-term investing works because you stay invested through the drops and participate in the recovery. Women who pull out during downturns lock in their losses and miss the recovery. Women who leave their investments alone and keep contributing through downturns almost always come out ahead.

## Step Seven: Learn as You Go

You do not need to understand everything before you start. Start with a Roth IRA and a single index fund. Learn more as you go. Increase your contributions as your income grows.

Some women add individual stocks later, once they are comfortable. Others add real estate investing or dividend stocks. All of that comes later. The Roth IRA and the index fund is the beginning and for many women it is all they ever need.

The best investment you will ever make is the one you start today instead of waiting until you feel ready. You will never feel fully ready. Start anyway.
